# Ideal Client Profile (ICP) & Qualification Framework {Pishi Studio}

## 1. Ideal Client Profile

### Target Client Types
Pishi Studio is best aligned with:
- professional service firms
- consulting practices
- specialized B2B service providers
- high-end design, architecture, or advisory firms
- founder-led companies entering a growth, repositioning, or operational maturity phase

These clients are typically no longer looking for disconnected design deliverables.
They are seeking structural coherence across brand, website, conversion logic, content visibility, and operational infrastructure.

### Strong Fit Signals
The client is a strong fit when they show one or more of the following:
- they have grown, but their digital presence no longer reflects the quality of their business
- their website, CRM, lead handling, messaging, and internal systems feel disconnected
- they want a cleaner, more authoritative digital presence that supports premium positioning
- they recognize that the issue is not just visual, but structural
- they are open to diagnosis, strategic thinking, and phased implementation

### Typical Problem Statements
Examples of strong-fit language:
- "We have grown, but our digital presence feels outdated or inconsistent."
- "Our website, CRM, proposals, and follow-up systems do not work together."
- "We need more clarity and structure across our digital presence."
- "Our current setup does not support premium positioning."
- "We want a clean, minimal system that feels credible and scalable."
- "We need better lead capture, follow-up, and automation logic."
- "Our brand looks one way, but our operations and website say something else."

### Engagement Mindset
Ideal clients:
- respect strategic architecture over quick marketing tactics
- value calm authority, precision, and long-term thinking
- are willing to begin with diagnosis before jumping into execution
- understand that coherence creates authority, efficiency, and scalability
- are looking for a high-trust strategic partner, not a task vendor

### Weak Fit Signals
The client is likely a weak fit when they:
- want isolated execution with no strategic context
- ask only for ads, SEO packages, logo tweaks, or one-off production tasks
- use highly transactional or price-driven language
- want urgent low-cost output without clarity
- are seeking free advice, speculative work, or exposure-based collaboration

## 2. Qualification Logic {Agent Scoring}

The agent must evaluate each lead using three criteria.
Each criterion is scored from 0 to 3.
Total score = 0 to 9.

### A. Professional Maturity & Scale {0–3}
Assess the maturity, clarity, and seriousness of the business.

- 3 = established firm, expert-led practice, or highly credible B2B business with clear positioning and strong professional signals
- 2 = growing service provider or founder-led company with clear commercial intent and a reasonably defined offer
- 1 = early-stage business, solo freelancer, or vague commercial positioning
- 0 = personal project, student work, non-serious inquiry, or spam

Signals that increase this score:
- clear business model
- professional website or company presence
- team, retained clients, or established service structure
- credible market positioning

### B. Problem Alignment {0–3}
Assess whether the request matches Pishi Studio’s structural architecture focus.

- 3 = explicitly structural request involving digital fragmentation, website architecture, conversion logic, CRM, automation, or ecosystem coherence
- 2 = meaningful brand/website/system concern, but not yet fully expressed in structural terms
- 1 = isolated tactical request with little evidence of system thinking
- 0 = irrelevant request or clear mismatch with studio scope

High-alignment examples:
- redesign website architecture
- unify brand and digital presence
- improve authority and conversion structure
- integrate CRM and follow-up systems
- build automation ecosystem
- resolve fragmented digital experience

Low-alignment examples:
- write one blog post
- run ads only
- quick logo fix
- backlink package
- generic marketing support

### C. Budget & Mindset Indicator {0–3}
Assess the seriousness of the client’s mindset, investment readiness, and strategic orientation.

- 3 = explicitly values long-term systems, authority, clarity, quality, or shows strong budget readiness
- 2 = professional and realistic, understands process, and appears open to a diagnostic or phased engagement
- 1 = transactional language, low-budget framing, urgency without depth, or unrealistic timeline pressure
- 0 = seeks free advice, speculative work, exposure partnerships, or clearly unsuitable terms

Signals that increase this score:
- openness to process
- respect for strategy before execution
- realistic expectations
- thoughtful problem framing
- willingness to invest in durable infrastructure

## 4. Service Tier Routing Rules

The agent must use these exact tier labels only:
- "Architecture"
- "Diagnostic"
- "Automation Add-on"
- "None"

### Routing Logic
- If Agent Score = "Red" → Proposed Tier = "None"
- If Agent Score = "Amber" → Proposed Tier = "Diagnostic"
- If Agent Score = "Green":
  - choose "Architecture" if the need is broad, systemic, and touches positioning, website structure, conversion logic, visibility, and governance
  - choose "Automation Add-on" if the need is primarily infrastructure-based and centered on CRM, lead routing, follow-up logic, or automations without requiring a full architecture engagement

---

## 5. Agent Interpretation Rules

The agent should prioritize structural signals over surface-level design language.

For example:
- "We want a premium-looking site" alone is not enough for a top-tier fit
- "Our business has outgrown our website and client journey" is a stronger signal
- "We need a better CRM and follow-up system" may qualify for "Automation Add-on" if the business itself is credible and the need is clearly operational

The agent must look for:
- seriousness
- structural need
- long-term orientation
- fit with a selective strategic consulting model

The agent must avoid over-scoring leads that sound polished but are actually asking for low-leverage execution.

---

## 6. Strategic Fit Principle

Pishi Studio does not optimize for lead volume.
It optimizes for structural fit, authority alignment, and high-trust engagement.

A qualified lead is not simply someone who needs a website.
A qualified lead is someone whose business would materially benefit from a more coherent digital architecture.
